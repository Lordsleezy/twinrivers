When a retaining wall is the right move

If soil is creeping toward a fence, a patio sits below grade, or a hillside leaves no flat play area, a retaining wall solves the grade problem instead of fighting it with taller posts alone.

Walls that work with fencing

We plan wall height, footing, and drainage so a fence can mount cleanly above or behind the wall. That coordination prevents leaning lines and washouts at the post line after the first heavy season.

Materials and site planning

Material choice depends on height, load, and look—block systems and timber options are common for residential yards. We assess soil, water flow, and access before recommending a system.

Service area

Local project examples

On a downhill backyard in Folsom we built a segmental block retaining wall to create a flat pad for a patio, then set the new wood fence posts above the wall cap with drainage gravel behind the face so winter storms would not push on the fence line. The wall height was chosen to keep soil off the neighbor’s fence while still allowing a six-foot privacy run on the rebuilt flat section.

Near Auburn we replaced a failing timber wall that was bowing into a side yard access path. The new system included perforated drain pipe and outlet to daylight, then a compact gate opening at the top of the wall so equipment could still reach the upper garden beds. Properties in Grass Valley and Nevada City often need similar drainage planning where spring runoff cuts channels before dry summer months. We flag those drainage paths during the first walk so the wall and fence quote covers the whole slope, not just the visible bow.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a retaining wall before installing a fence?

Not always. Mild slopes can often be stepped with the fence. When soil is moving or the grade drop is sharp, a wall is the stable long-term fix.

Can you build a wall and fence together?

Yes. Combining scopes usually saves mobilization cost and ensures the fence posts and wall drainage are planned as one system.

How tall can a residential retaining wall be?

Height limits and engineering needs vary by city and load. We advise what is typical for your site during the on-site visit.

Do retaining walls need drainage?

Proper drainage behind the wall is critical. We plan gravel and drain paths so water does not pressure the face after storms.
