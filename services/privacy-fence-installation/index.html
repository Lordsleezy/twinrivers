Privacy Fence Installation

A solid privacy fence turns your backyard into a true outdoor room. Twin Rivers LLC installs board-on-board cedar, cap-and-trim wood lines, and vinyl privacy systems with heights and setbacks planned for California codes and HOA rules. Explore privacy fence installation by city below.

Privacy fence installation process

Privacy projects demand accurate layout because every inch affects sight lines into neighbors' yards. We verify lines, discuss height limits, and mark gates for mower and trash access before digging.

Posts are typically closer spaced and heavier at corners to resist wind on solid panels. Wood privacy gets board-on-board or overlap patterns to minimize gaps as boards dry. Vinyl privacy uses reinforced posts at gates. We finish with hardware adjustments and a walkthrough of maintenance expectations.

Wood and vinyl privacy options

Wood privacy fences use cedar or redwood boards with optional top cap and trim for a finished look. Heights of 6 ft and 8 ft are common in rear yards; front-yard privacy must respect city and HOA visibility rules.

Vinyl privacy panels offer a uniform appearance without gap creep. Lattice toppers can soften height transitions. For noise dampening, mass and tight board spacing help; we discuss realistic expectations on busy streets.

Privacy fencing across our region

Dense neighborhoods in Sacramento, Citrus Heights, and Elk Grove rely on 6-foot privacy for pools and pets. Roseville and Rocklin often combine front decorative fencing with rear privacy wood or vinyl. Windy corridors in Lincoln and Folsom need robust corner posts on long privacy runs.

In Grass Valley and Nevada City, stepped privacy on slopes preserves height without uneven gaps at the bottom. We install in these cities regularly—local knowledge reduces surprises on install day.

Frequently asked questions

What is the maximum privacy fence height in my city?

Rear yards often allow 6 or 8 feet; front yards are usually lower. Rules vary—we apply typical standards for your city and ask you to confirm HOA docs if applicable.

Wood or vinyl for privacy?

Wood offers natural look and easy height customization; vinyl offers low maintenance. Budget, HOA, and desired appearance drive the choice—we show samples on site.

Will a privacy fence block all noise?

Solid fences reduce some street noise but are not soundproof. Tight board spacing and height help; we set realistic expectations for your street.

How do you prevent gaps between boards over time?

Board-on-board and proper drying time before install reduce visible gaps. We also explain seasonal movement so you know what is normal.

Planning backyard privacy

Privacy height should match how you use the yard— pool, hot tub, and second-story sight lines all matter. We walk the property with you to see what neighbors can view from upper windows, not just ground level.

Setbacks from sidewalks and greenbelts vary; Roseville and Rocklin plats often include drainage easements where fence placement must stop short. We flag those before digging.

Board-on-board costs more than single-sided privacy but hides gaps as boards shrink. If you prefer vinyl, we discuss wind load on long solid runs and where reinforcement posts belong.

Gates in privacy lines should be framed with the same rigidity as the field— we never hang a heavy privacy gate on an unstiffened post. Plan walk and equipment gates now so the finished yard functions daily.

Scheduling and next steps
