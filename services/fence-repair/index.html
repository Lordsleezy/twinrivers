Fence Repair

Wind, wet clay, and years of sun beat up fence lines across Northern California. Twin Rivers LLC repairs wood, vinyl, and chain link fences with a focus on posts and structure first—so the fix lasts past the next storm season. We serve homeowners, landlords, and commercial sites from Sacramento to Grass Valley.

Our fence repair process

Every repair starts with a walk of the failed run. We note which posts are still solid at grade, whether rails or panels can be salvaged, and how the gate hardware is wearing. You get a written scope before work begins—no surprise add-ons after we are on site.

For leaning sections we often extract or sister posts, reset depth for your soil, and plumb the line before reattaching panels. Partial panel replacement is matched as closely as your existing material allows. When a repair would cost nearly as much as a new span, we say so plainly and quote both options.

What we fix and the materials we use

We repair cedar, redwood, pressure-treated wood, vinyl privacy systems, and galvanized chain link. Common jobs include replacing rotten post bases, rebuilding corners that carry wind load, re-hanging sagging gates, swapping broken pickets or rails, and securing loose footings in expansive clay.

Hardware matters: we use hinges, latches, and screws suited for outdoor use so gates stay aligned. For vinyl we source compatible rails and posts from major manufacturers when possible. Chain link repairs include new tension wire, ties, and fabric patches that blend with the existing mesh.

Local service area

Our crews repair fences throughout the Sacramento Valley and western Sierra Foothills. Sacramento and Elk Grove properties often see post heave in heavy clay after winter rains. Frequently asked questions

Can you repair only one section of my fence?

Yes. We isolate the failed posts or panels and rebuild that span while leaving sound sections in place, matching materials as closely as practical.

Why does my fence lean after rain?

Central Valley clay expands when wet and shrinks when dry, loosening shallow posts. We reset posts deeper or improve footings so the line stays plumb.

Do you repair vinyl and chain link fences?

Yes. We fix vinyl rails, posts, and gates where parts are available, and we patch or retension chain link fabric and gates.

How fast can you schedule fence repair?

Many repairs are booked within a few days. Storm damage and open lines that affect security or pets are prioritized when possible.

Getting an accurate fence repair quote

Bring photos of leaning posts, broken boards, and gate gaps when you call—they help us load the right lumber or vinyl parts on the first trip. We still verify post rot at grade in person because that determines whether a partial fix is worth doing.

For rental turnovers, we itemize board counts, post replacements, and haul-off separately so owners can approve line by line. Homeowners get the same written scope to compare repair versus replacing a full side of the yard.

If a neighbor shares the fence line, tell us before install day so we can plan access and avoid surprises. We work with shared-line etiquette common in Sacramento and Placer County neighborhoods every week.

Emergency storm work may be triaged by safety first: securing pets, closing open spans, then scheduling permanent rebuilds. Mention downed trees or power lines so we know whether utility clearance is required before our crew arrives.

Scheduling and next steps

Most fence repairs begin with a short site visit or detailed photos if you are out of town managing a rental. We confirm material lead times—especially for specialty vinyl profiles—before locking your date so the crew is not waiting on parts.

After repair, we point out maintenance habits that extend life: keeping soil off wood boards, adjusting gate hinges seasonally, and trimming vegetation that traps moisture on posts. Small habits prevent the same failure mode from repeating on the next span.
