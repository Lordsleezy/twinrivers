Chain Link Fence Installation

Chain link is durable, economical, and easy to see through—ideal for pet runs, side yards, and commercial perimeters. Twin Rivers LLC installs galvanized and vinyl-coated chain link with tensioned fabric, secure posts, and gates sized for your equipment. Select your city below for local install details.

Chain link installation process

We layout the run, set terminal posts and line posts at engineered spacing, and pour footings suited to soil. Top rail is threaded through fittings for a straight, rattling-free line. Fabric is stretched with a come-along to the correct tension and tied consistently.

Gates are hung with adjustable hinges and latches; wider openings get anti-sag kits. We coordinate with existing structures, block walls, and property pins so the fence follows your legal line.

Mesh, coating, and heights

Standard galvanized mesh resists rust in most backyard uses. Vinyl-coated green or black chain link blends into landscaping. Common heights are 4 ft for front yards, 6 ft for side and rear privacy-adjacent runs, and taller options for commercial security.

We install barbed or razor wire only where legally appropriate on commercial jobs. Slats can be added for partial privacy. Post sizes increase with height and wind exposure.

Frequently asked questions

How long does chain link fence last?

Galvanized chain link often lasts decades with minimal care. Coated fabric may show wear at ties first; we use quality fittings and correct tension to maximize life.

Can chain link be installed on a slope?

Yes. We rack fabric or step the line depending on grade and your aesthetic preference.

Do you install chain link gates for RV access?

Yes. We size double gates and posts for the opening you need and reinforce hinges for heavier daily use.

Is chain link allowed in my front yard?

Rules vary by city and HOA. We advise on typical front-yard visibility requirements in your municipality during the estimate.

Planning chain link layout and access

Chain link shines when you need security without blocking sight lines. Tell us about pets that dig, because we can add bottom tension wire or concrete mow strips at the base.

Vehicle gates need flat approach pads or graded entries; we check grade with you so the gate does not scrape after the first rain. RV and trailer widths drive post size and hinge rating— share exact dimensions.

Commercial jobs may need multiple pedestrian gates; we place them for OSHA-style egress paths when you describe worker flow. Residential side yards often combine a 3-foot walk gate with a wider double at the rear.

If you might upgrade to wood or vinyl later, we can set posts on the correct line now so future panels align without moving footings.

Scheduling and next steps

Chain link projects can proceed in wider weather windows than stain-dependent wood work, but concrete still needs reasonable set time before tensioning fabric hard. We sequence terminal posts, line posts, then stretch day for the quietest, tightest result.

If you share the property survey or corner pins, we align the fence to legal boundaries rather than old lines that may be encroaching. That conversation saves neighbor disputes later.
