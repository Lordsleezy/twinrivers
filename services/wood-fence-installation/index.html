Wood Fence Installation

Wood fences remain the most popular choice for privacy and natural character in Northern California yards. Twin Rivers LLC installs cedar, redwood, and pressure-treated lines with posts set for local clay and rock, clean alignment, and gates that swing true. Browse by city below or call for a site estimate.

Wood fence installation process

We start with a site walk: property lines, utilities, slope, and gate locations. You choose board orientation, height, and cap style; we confirm what your city or HOA typically allows for front and side yards.

Posts are dug and set to depth for your soil—deeper in soft clay, with rock drilling in foothill lots. Rails are leveled, boards are fastened with corrosion-resistant hardware, and gates are framed separately so they stay square. We leave the site broom-clean and walk the line with you at completion.

Wood species and board options

Cedar and redwood offer natural rot resistance and accept stain well for a long service life. Pressure-treated pine is economical for rear-yard privacy when properly sealed. We build board-on-board privacy, flat-top cap, dog-ear, and shadowbox styles.

Finish choices include transparent stain, solid color, or leaving natural gray weathering—many customers pair new installs with our fence staining service. We advise on spacing, post size (4x4 vs 6x6 at corners), and kick boards for pet yards.

Sacramento Valley and foothill installs

Wood fence installs in Sacramento and Elk Grove must account for expansive clay and long dry summers. Roseville and Rocklin often require HOA-matched heights and stain colors. Folsom and Lincoln lots along greenbelts need wind-rated corners. In Auburn, Grass Valley, and Nevada City, rock and grade changes drive post footings and stepped runs.

Our local crews schedule efficiently because we are already working in these cities weekly—not flying in from out of state.

Frequently asked questions

Cedar or redwood—which is better near Sacramento?

Both perform well. Cedar is widely available; redwood offers excellent durability. Budget and HOA color rules often drive the choice—we explain tradeoffs on site.

How long does wood fence installation take?

Typical backyard privacy lines take one to three days on site after materials arrive. Longer runs, rock, or multiple gates add time.

Do you remove old wood fencing?

Yes, removal and haul-off can be included in your scope so you only pay for what the property needs.

When should I stain a new wood fence?

Many new boards benefit from waiting a few weeks to dry, then staining or sealing. We offer staining services and can schedule follow-up work.

Planning your wood fence layout

Mark where you want trash, mower, and pedestrian access before we stake the line. Moving a gate after posts are set is expensive; a few minutes of layout saves hundreds later.

Corner posts and end posts carry most of the load— we upsize them on long straight runs and windy corridors common near Folsom Lake and open fields in Lincoln. Kick boards help dog yards and reduce soil splash on the first board row.

If you are replacing an old fence, show us photos of what failed. Shallow posts or wrong fastener choice tells us what to improve on the new line.

Stain and seal schedules can be bundled after the wood dries. Many customers schedule staining a few weeks post-install for best absorption— ask during your estimate.

Scheduling and next steps

Wood fence installs depend on material delivery and weather for concrete set times. We communicate pour dates and gate curing times so you know when pets can return to the yard safely.

We recommend walking the finished line with us to confirm height, cap details, and gate swing before final payment. That walkthrough prevents misunderstandings about intentional gaps or drainage clearances at the bottom board.
