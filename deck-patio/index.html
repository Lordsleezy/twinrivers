Decks built for California living

We frame decks for level living space off the house or over uneven grade, with attention to footings, ledger details, and railings where required. Materials and finishes are chosen for sun exposure and how you entertain outdoors.

Patios and yard transitions

Patio projects focus on usable flat space, clean transitions to lawn or garden beds, and gate access that does not fight furniture layouts. When a fence is part of the plan, we align openings and sight lines with the patio edge.

One crew for fence, gate, and deck

Hiring separate trades for fence and deck often leaves gaps in scheduling and elevation details. Twin Rivers can scope fencing, gates, and deck work together so heights, steps, and access match on day one.

Local project examples

In Sacramento we framed a low deck off a sliding door with a new cedar privacy fence wrapping two sides of the platform, so railing height and fence height were coordinated for the same sight line from the kitchen. Footings were placed to miss an existing irrigation main, and a gate was sized for a grill cart without narrowing the stair landing.

A Roseville patio project started with compacting base rock for pavers, then tying a short fence section into the patio edge so pets could not cut across new planting beds. In Auburn, we stepped deck framing down a moderate slope and aligned fence panels with each tread so the transition from deck to yard did not leave awkward gaps or extra posts in the walking path. Combined deck-and-fence scopes like these are common when families want one outdoor level for seating and a secure perimeter for pets.

Frequently asked questions

Can you build a deck and fence in the same project?

Yes. Combined projects are common and usually more efficient. We sequence footings, framing, and fence posts so nothing blocks access mid-build.

Do decks need permits?

Many decks do, depending on height and city rules. We discuss typical requirements for your location during the estimate.

What deck materials do you recommend?

It depends on budget, sun exposure, and maintenance preference. We review options on site and price the system that fits how you use the space.

Do you only build decks with new fences?

No. We can add a deck or patio where fencing already exists, or repair fence lines that meet the new structure.
