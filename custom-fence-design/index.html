Design that starts on site

We walk the property, note grade changes, drainage, trees, utilities, and sight lines. Then we recommend heights, materials, and gate placements that solve privacy and access without fighting the lot.

Mixed materials and specialty details

Custom work can include board-on-board privacy, lattice tops, picture-frame trim, transition heights, decorative face boards, or combining wood privacy in back with open styles in front. Commercial custom work focuses on security, traffic flow, and durable hardware.

HOA and neighborhood standards

Many Sacramento Valley and foothill neighborhoods have style or height rules. We help you choose compliant designs and provide the clear scope drawings or descriptions HOAs often ask for.

From design to install

Once the design is set, the same crew builds it—posts, panels, gates, and finish details—so the plan does not get lost between a designer and a separate installer.

Local project examples

For a corner lot in Lincoln we designed a stepped privacy fence that drops from six feet along the street to four feet toward the front porch, with picture-frame trim and a lattice top where the HOA allows visibility at the sidewalk. The layout kept the backyard fully private while avoiding a solid wall that would have blocked light into the living room windows.

In Nevada City we planned a mixed-material line: tight cedar privacy along the creek side for deer pressure, then an open horizontal slat section toward the driveway for airflow and views. Gate placement was driven by where snow loads slide off the roof in winter, so the primary walk gate sits outside the drip line and a secondary gate opens to a gravel path rather than lawn. That kind of site-specific planning is what keeps custom fences usable year-round instead of fighting the lot.

Frequently asked questions

Can you match a neighbor’s fence style?

We can usually match height, board pattern, and color closely. Exact vintage materials are not always available, but we aim for a cohesive look along the shared line.

Do custom fences cost more than standard installs?

Specialty trim, mixed heights, and complex slopes add labor and materials. Many custom jobs are only modestly above a standard privacy run when the layout is straightforward.

Can you design around a pool or slope?

Yes. Pool codes and hillside steps need specific planning. We design for code, drainage, and safe access before installation starts.

Do you provide design options before I commit?

Yes. After the site visit we outline material and layout options with pricing so you can choose before we schedule the build.
