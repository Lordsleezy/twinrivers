Who we are

Twin Rivers LLC is a local crew that spends most days on fences—new installs, storm-beaten repairs, and gates that stopped latching. We’re used to rocky soil, tight side yards, HOAs asking for specifics, and that one post that heaves every time the ground saturates.

We pick materials and hardware for how your yard actually behaves: sun exposure, slope, pets, kids, and how much privacy you want from the street or the neighbor’s deck. You get a clear plan before we dig, and a fence you’re not babysitting every season.

You’ll see us from Sacramento and Elk Grove up through Roseville, Rocklin, and Folsom, and into the foothills around Auburn, Grass Valley, and Nevada City. If you’re nearby and need a straight answer on what’s worth fixing versus replacing, we’re easy to reach.

Call or send a note when you’re ready—we’ll look at photos or swing by, give you an honest scope, and get you scheduled.

What we do

Fence installation

New perimeter and backyard fences—wood privacy, picket, vinyl, chain link for pets or pools, and layouts that follow your property line without guesswork.

Fence repair

Leaning sections, rotted posts, blown panels after wind, and hardware that’s given up. We fix what’s salvageable and tell you when a full replace is the smarter spend.

Gate installation

Single and double gates, latches that line up, and openings wide enough for your trailer or trash bins—manual setups and automation when it fits the job.

Property improvements

Decks, yard tweaks, and exterior fixes for homes and rentals—work that pairs with a solid fence so the whole place feels finished.

Questions we get a lot

Is your install work covered if something shifts?

Yes—we warranty our workmanship so you’re not on your own if something wasn’t done right. Material coverage follows the manufacturer; we’ll spell out what that means on your job.

Can you do a wider driveway gate or automation?

We hang manual and automated gates, set posts deep enough for the weight, and make sure clearance and swing work with your slope and apron.

How soon can you fix a fence that’s about to fall?

Most repairs slot in within a few days once we know the footage and post count. If we need a specific panel or style, lead time depends on what’s in stock locally.
