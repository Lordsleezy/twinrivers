Why stain and seal matter here

UV breaks down lignin in wood; rain and irrigation soak end grain and posts at grade. A quality stain with sealer slows both problems. Skipping protection is one of the fastest ways a new wood fence greys, cups, and needs early board replacement.

What the service includes

We prep the surface as needed, protect nearby landscaping, apply stain evenly across boards and rails, and treat the faces that take the most sun. Color options range from natural cedar tones to deeper browns that hide wear between refresh cycles.

Best timing for Northern California

Dry, mild days are ideal—common in spring and fall. Fresh installs should cure before coating; we advise timing during your quote so stain bonds correctly.

Pair staining with repairs

If boards are already failing, we can repair or replace damaged sections first, then stain the full line so new wood matches. That combination often costs less than waiting until the whole fence needs replacement.

Local project examples

On a south-facing cedar line in Rocklin, we cleaned grey oxidation, replaced a handful of cupped boards at the bottom, then applied a semi-transparent stain with UV blockers so the natural grain stayed visible. That yard gets full afternoon sun, so we scheduled the coat in early fall when temperatures were steady and irrigation could stay off the fence line for the cure window. Two seasons later the boards still hold color on the street-facing side.

A Grass Valley customer hired us to stain a new redwood install the same season it went in, plus refresh an older gate that had never been sealed. We masked stone planters, back-brushed end grain on posts, and used a slightly darker tone on the gate so scuffs from snow shovels would not show as quickly through the winter. The owner now has a written maintenance interval matched to foothill freeze-thaw cycles.

Frequently asked questions

How often should a wood fence be stained?

Many Sacramento Valley fences benefit from a refresh every few years, depending on sun exposure and product. South- and west-facing runs usually need attention sooner.

Can you stain an older grey fence?

Often yes after prep. Heavily weathered wood may need cleaning or board replacement first so the stain absorbs evenly.

Is staining included with a new fence install?

Staining is typically a separate scope. Many homeowners add it at install or within the first season for maximum protection.

Do you stain vinyl fences?

Vinyl does not need traditional stain. For vinyl we focus on cleaning and hardware care; staining services are for wood fences.
