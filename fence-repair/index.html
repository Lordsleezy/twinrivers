Common fence repairs we handle

We reset leaning posts, replace rotten sections, rebuild blown panels, rehang sagging gates, swap broken rails, and reinforce corners that carry wind load. If only part of the line failed, we match materials as closely as practical so the repair does not look patched.

Repair vs. replace—clear recommendations

If posts are still solid and only a few panels failed, repair usually wins. If posts are soft at grade for most of the run, a new fence is often the better investment. You get that call before we start, with photos and a written scope.

Storm and emergency fence work

After high winds or tree damage, we can secure open sections, rebuild the failed run, and restore gate access. Fast response matters for pets, kids, and property lines—call (916) 906-2254 when something is down.

Local project examples

After a January wind event in Rocklin, we rebuilt forty feet of blown-down wood privacy where three posts had rotted at grade but the rest of the run was still sound. Instead of quoting a full replacement, we replaced those posts with longer embedment, sistered new rails, and matched board spacing so the repaired span blends with the original fence facing the greenbelt.

In Citrus Heights we fixed a sagging driveway gate that had pulled its hinge post loose in expansive clay. The repair included a new concrete footing, heavy hinges, and a drop rod on the double leaf so the latch line stayed true. Rental owners in Sacramento call us for quick panel swaps and post resets between tenants when dogs or landscaping equipment damage one section without taking down the whole yard.

Frequently asked questions

Can you repair just one section of my fence?

Yes. Partial repairs are common. We isolate the failed posts or panels and rebuild that span so the rest of the fence stays in place.

Why is my fence leaning after rain?

Central Valley clay expands and softens, and shallow posts lose grip. We reset posts deeper or with better footings so the line stays plumb through wet seasons.

Do you repair vinyl and chain link as well as wood?

Yes. We repair wood, vinyl, and chain link fences, including gates and hardware, as long as replacement parts are available for your system.

How quickly can you schedule a repair?

Many repairs are scheduled within days. Storm damage and open property lines are prioritized when possible.
